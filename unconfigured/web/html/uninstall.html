Uninstalling %%PROJECT_NAME%%

Before you uninstall, take a minute to consider if you want to uninstall or just pause the logging software for some amount of time. You have the ability to pause the logger, which will prevent it from logging data and notifying you of available experiments. For more information about pausing, see the "Pausing the Logger" section of the help page.

Uninstall %%PROJECT_NAME%% for:

  • Google Chrome
  • Firefox 3.*
  • Firefox 4.*

After uninstalling, you may join the study again at a later time if you would like by re-installing the extension.

Uninstalling %%PROJECT_NAME%% for Google Chrome

To uninstall %%PROJECT_NAME%% for Chrome, right click the pause/play button on the location bar. This will bring up a Chrome menu. Click on the "Uninstall" option to uninstall the extension. Here is a screen shot of the menu that pops up:



Uninstalling %%PROJECT_NAME%% for Firefox 3.*

Perform the following steps:

1. Go to "Tools"->"Add-ons".

2. A window will pop up. Select "Extensions" at the top. This will look different depending on your operating system.

3. Find and select "%%PROJECT_NAME%%" in the list of installed extensions. Click on the "Uninstall" button and Firefox will uninstall the extension. You will then be prompted to restart to make the change permanent.



Uninstalling %%PROJECT_NAME%% for Firefox 4.*

Perform the following steps:

1. Open the Add-ons page—this is done differently based on operating system:

1a. If you are using Windows 7, click the "Firefox" menu in the upper left and select "Add-ons"

1b. Otherwise, you should have a "Tools" menu; go to "Tools"->"Add-ons".

1c. You can also enter the URL about:addons.

2. A new tab will be displayed. Select "Extensions" on the left.

3. Find and select the "%%PROJECT_NAME%%" in the list of installed extensions. Click on the "Remove" button and Firefox will uninstall the extension. You will then be prompted to restart to make the change permanent.
